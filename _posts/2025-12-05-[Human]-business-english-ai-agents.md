## ğŸ“š Grammar Focus: Future Continuous vs. Future Perfect â³

In business news, we talk a lot about **predictions**. Two tenses are very important here!

### 1ï¸âƒ£ Future Continuous (Will be + -ing)
Use this for an action that will be **in progress** at a specific time in the future.

<div class="grammar-box">
  <strong style="color: #00d9ff;">Formula:</strong> Subject + will be + verb-ing<br>
  <strong style="color: #ffd93d;">Meaning:</strong> "In the middle of doing something"<br><br>
  
  âœ… "By 2026, we <strong>will be using</strong> AI agents daily."<br>
  âœ… "Next year, the company <strong>will be expanding</strong> to Asia."
</div>

### 2ï¸âƒ£ Future Perfect (Will have + Past Participle)
Use this for an action that will be **finished/completed** *before* a specific time in the future.

<div class="grammar-box">
  <strong style="color: #6bcb77;">Formula:</strong> Subject + will have + V3 (Past Participle)<br>
  <strong style="color: #ffd93d;">Meaning:</strong> "Done before that time"<br><br>
  
  âœ… "By 2030, AI <strong>will have transformed</strong> the workforce."<br>
  âœ… "By next month, we <strong>will have finished</strong> the project."
</div>

### ğŸ†š Comparison Example:

*   **Future Continuous:** "At 9 AM tomorrow, I **will be writing** the report." (I am busy writing it).
*   **Future Perfect:** "By 5 PM tomorrow, I **will have written** the report." (It is finished!).
