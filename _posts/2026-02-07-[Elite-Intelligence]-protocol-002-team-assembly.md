To build an Elite Intelligence cell, you don't look for "employees." You look for **Assets**. Inspired by the high-pressure environments of Unit 8200 and Mossad, this protocol outlines the exact setup for your team.

## üë• Phase I: The Archetypes (Who to Recruit)

Don't hire for "Years of Experience." Hire for **Cognitive Agility**. Assign your current team members to these archetypes based on their natural instincts:

<div class="persona-card">
    <h4>1. The "Katsa" (Case Officer) ‚Äî Desk A / Desk D</h4>
    <p><strong>Trait:</strong> High empathy + High manipulation + Extreme clarity.</p>
    <p><strong>Role:</strong> They talk to people, gather human intel (HUMINT), and package the final output. If you need to convince a client or a stakeholder, this is your operator.</p>
</div>

<div class="persona-card">
    <h4>2. The "Yakhalam" (The Specialist) ‚Äî Desk C</h4>
    <p><strong>Trait:</strong> Deep technical obsession. They don't sleep until the code works.</p>
    <p><strong>Role:</strong> They are the builders. They take the "impossible" idea from intelligence and turn it into a working prototype in 48 hours.</p>
</div>

<div class="persona-card">
    <h4>3. The "Tenth Man" (The Contrarian) ‚Äî Desk B</h4>
    <p><strong>Trait:</strong> Low agreeableness + Extremely high logic.</p>
    <p><strong>Role:</strong> Their sole job is to tell you why you are wrong. They must be protected by the leader. If they stop criticizing, your intelligence is failing.</p>
</div>

A team is just a group of people until they have a **Rhythm**. Set this up tomorrow:

<div class="tactical-box">

    <div class="check-item">
